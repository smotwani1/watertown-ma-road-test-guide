Massachusetts road test

The Watertown RMV road test, without surprises.

The requirements, the maneuvers, both routes, and the rules people get wrong — so on test day, the only nerves you feel are the normal kind.

At a glance

Where
Watertown RMV, 550 Arsenal St., Watertown, MA 02472. Official The test starts in the lot behind the building — look for the "RMV road test" sign. Reports
Bring
A completed Class D Road Test Application, your legible original learner's permit, and (if under 18) a parent or guardian's signature. Official
Sponsor
21+, at least one year of driving experience, a valid U.S. license (no foreign licenses), and the physical license on hand — not a photo. Official
The car
The examiner must be able to reach the parking brake for an emergency stop. If it isn't part of the console, or access is blocked, the car can't be used. Needs valid registration and a current inspection sticker. Official
If under 18
40 hours of supervised driving — or 30 if you finished an RMV-approved skills program — on top of driver's ed. Official
Fee
$35, not refunded if you fail, show up unprepared, lack a registered vehicle, arrive late, or cancel within 72 hours. Official

Before you pull out

Watertown examiners start the same way every time. Bank these easy points.

  • The opening drill. You'll demonstrate your hand signals (left, right, stop), show that your signals work, and beep the horn. Reports
  • Order matters. Buckle up and adjust the rearview and both side mirrors before you start the engine — examiners watch the sequence. Reports
  • The hill question. You may be asked which way to turn your wheels on a slope. Memory aid: UCLA — Uphill, Curb, Left, Always; everything else points toward the curb. Official

Your day-of plan

  • You'll know both routes, the maneuvers, and exactly what's scored. That certainty is the real antidote to test-day nerves.
  • "Slow is smooth, smooth is fast." Deliberate driving looks competent. Breathe, eyes forward, gentle on the brake.
  • Arrive 15 minutes early, practice both routes in your test car beforehand, and — when you finish — put the car in park.

If you don't pass

Plenty of people don't pass the first time — it's a points system, not a verdict on you. You'll wait two weeks before retaking, and you can attempt up to six road tests in a 12-month period. Rebook online, then re-drill whatever cost you points. Official
