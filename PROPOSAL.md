## Client Brief

**Client:** Akshata Kapoor, my girlffriend. Just passed her Massachusetts theory test and has obtained her learners permit. She is now gearing up for her road test at the Watertown MA RMV. She tends to get quite anxious furing tests and hence she requires a medium that perpares her and calms her nerves for the test. She is going to be a real user for a website that helps her to ace her road test, I am building this for her based on her needs.

**Purpose:** A free, in depth guide that shows what a Watertown test-taker exactly what to expect such as insider info like routes, maneuvers that are usually tested, scored items, and car requirements, so test-day anxiety comes from normal nerves, not the unknown.

**Audience:** Learners permit holders who are taking their Class D road test (and parents of these learners permit holders) taking the road test at the Watertown RMV soon. They arrive anxious and under-informed and need to know what the test involves, the possible routes, how each maneuver is graded, and the official requirements. Akshata is an ideal representation of this audience.

**Key action:** Study and absorb the guide before test day, learn the maneuvers that are asked at this SPECIFIC RMV and its routes and confirm if the car qualifies for a road test. Because she's a visual learner who studies by active recall, visual cues will be incorporated to better help her understand especially with routes and maneuvers like parallel parking.

**Pages/sections needed:**
- At a glance (location, what to bring, sponsor rules, car/parking-brake requirement, fee)
- Before you pull out (hand signals + mirror sequence)
- What the examiner is grading (the scored checklist, the centerpiece)
- The maneuvers, step by step (parallel park, three-point turn, 50-ft reverse, hill parking)
- The two Watertown routes (with visual route cues)
- Speed zones & the right-on-red trap
- Top reasons people fail
- Rules people get wrong
- Day-of plan / if you don't pass
- Flashcard self-quiz (added from the interview)

**Content status:** Largely complete, sourced from official MA RMV documents, the Driver's Manual, and corroborated test-taker accounts. Still needed: the footer contact link and dates, plus the flashcard self-quiz and stronger visual route cues the interview surfaced.

**Style preferences:** Visual learner, she used Zutobi for her permit test and relied on its flashcards and video/visual cues, retaining information far better that way than from long text. Calm and reassuring, because she has test anxiety and said knowing the route in advance would steady her nerves (supports the warm "paper" background, soft serif headings, and gentle green accent). Wants depth and "insider" detail about what actually happens on the test like predetermined routes and maneuvers asked.

**Inspiration sites:**
- Zutobi (her primary reference) because she loves the flashcards and visual/video learning; keep the active-recall flashcard format, change it to be Watertown-specific and route-focused instead of generic
- "Driving Test Routes" app is good idea that people want their specific test route; change to free, deeper, and reassuring rather than a paid map line, and actually cover Watertown (it doesn't)
- RMV Drivers Test - Driving the Car a Youtube video because there is no specific website that covers the Watertown RMV road test which truly shows her what she will expirience and she wishes this generic RMV road test youtube video was specifically tailered to the Watertown RMV

## Layout Plan

- A Figma sketch of the single-page scroll structure is committed to the repo 
- Reference site to emulate: **Zutobi**, for its flashcard-driven, visual study approach. **Keep:** active-recall flashcards and visual cues she responds to. **Change:** make everything Watertown-specific, route-focused, free, and tuned to calm an anxious test-taker. (Key interview-driven change: the original was prose-only; because she learns visually via flashcards, I'm adding a flashcard self-quiz and visual route cues.)
- The written description of the layout I gave to AI as my starting prompt:

  > Build a single-page, mobile-first HTML guide for the Massachusetts road test at the Watertown RMV, aimed at an anxious learner driver who needs to know exactly what to expect. Tone: calm, reassuring, confidence-building, remove uncertainty so the only nerves are normal ones. Use a warm off-white "paper" background, a soft serif for headings (Fraunces), a clean sans for body (Hanken Grotesk), one gentle green accent, and dashed "road-lane" dividers between sections. Single-column, easy to skim on a phone the night before. Sections in order: hero with a one-line reassuring promise; an "At a glance" summary card (location, what to bring, sponsor rules, the car/parking-brake requirement, fee); the opening sequence before driving; a prominent "What the examiner is grading" scored checklist as the visual centerpiece; expandable step-by-step blocks for each maneuver (parallel park, three-point turn, 50-ft reverse, hill parking); the two Watertown routes as numbered walkthroughs with clear visual cues; speed zones and the right-on-red trap; top reasons people fail; a "rules people get wrong" Q and A; a flashcard self-quiz on the key rules for active-recall study; a day-of plan; and a sources footer. Mark every factual claim with its source, official (verified against RMV docs and the Driver's Manual) versus reported by recent test-takers, and never state route specifics as guaranteed, since routes can change.
